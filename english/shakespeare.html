Shakespeare's language is an example of what linguists call Early Modern English. There are many resources about Shakespeare's English: dictionaries, grammars, Shakespearean insult generators etcetera.

Original Pronunciation

The British linguist David Crystal did research on English pronunciation in Shakespeare's age. He published a book about it: Pronouncing Shakespeare (Cambridge University Press, 2005. ISBN 9780521852135), but there are also several YouTube videos about it.

  • Shakespeare: Original pronunciation is a video by the Open University in which the David Crystal and his son Ben Crystal, who is an actor, explain what original pronunciation is, what effect its use had in a 2004 Shakespeare production, and give a few examples.
  • Shakespeareâ€™s accent is a video by the British Council in which actor Ben Crystal explains what Original Pronunciation is and gives a few examples.
  • Speaking the bright and beautiful English of Shakespeare is a video by the British Council in which Ben Crystal explores the accent, the theatrical conventions, and the world of Shakespeare, to reveal a bright and beautiful English. (90 minutes)
