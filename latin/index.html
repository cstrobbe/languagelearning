Resources for Learning Latin

  • The Open University: Getting started on classical Latin, a free course hosted on the OpenLearn platform. It requires 10 hours of study.
  • Kostas Katsouranis: Latin Lessons (playlist on YouTube; focus on pronunciation).
  • Winfred P. Lehmann & Jonathan Slocum: Latin Online, The University of Texas at Austin, Linguistics Research Center.
  • Latin Tutorial (YouTube channel with many playlists that teach Latin; also some literature, e.g. The Aeneid).
  • NachhilfeLatein (German YouTube channel with many playlists, including one with 104 videos on Latin grammar, and many other ones on Latin literary texts).
  • MrLateinnachhilfe (another German YouTube channel; unfortunately, the videos have not been organised into playlists).
  • Education Scotland: Active learning strategies in classical languages.
  • The National Archives (UK): Beginners' Latin: Latin 1086 – 1733: a practical online tutorial for beginners: a tutorial for the Latin used between 1086 and 1733 in British documents.
  • Cornell College, Classical Studies: Tips for Learning Latin with Less Stress.
  • Allen, W. Sidney: Vox Latina: A Guide to the Pronunciation of Classical Latin. Second edition. Cambridge University Press, 1989. (148 pages) ISBN 9780521379366.
  • Jones, Peter; Sidwell, Keith: An Independent Study Guide to Reading Latin. Second edition. Cambridge University Press, 2018. (224 pages) ISBN 9781107615601.
  • Dickey, Eleanor: Learn Latin from the Romans: A Complete Introductory Course Using Textbooks from the Roman Empire. Cambridge University Press, 2018. (526 pages) ISBN 9781316506196.
  • Dickey, Eleanor: Learning Latin the Ancient Way: Latin Textbooks from the Ancient World. Cambridge University Press, 2016. (197 pages) ISBN 9781107474574.
  • Grandgent, Charles Hall: An Introduction to Vulgar Latin. 1907. The book is now in the public domain and available on various websites, such as Forgotten Books and the Internet Archive.
  • Textkit: Learn Greek and Latin!: a collection of links to resources for learning Greek and Latin, especially courses, grammar books and books on prose composition.
  • Cours de latin: series of 24 short YouTube videos (two to seven minutes) in French that cover mainly grammar.

Lingua Latina Per Se Illustrata

  • Lingua Latina Per Se Illustrata is a Latin course written by the Danish language teacher Hans Ørberg. It relies on images and Latin text (instead of translations) to teach Latin.
    The course itself consists of two volumes, and there are a few companion volumes that are not in Latin. (See the series homepage at Hackett Publishing.)
    • Ørberg, Hans: Pars I: Familia Romana.
    • Ørberg, Hans: Pars II: Roma Aeterna
    • Neumann, Jeanne Marie: Lingua Latina: A Companion to Familia Romana. Second edition, 2016.
    • Owens, Patrick M.: Lingua Latina: Pars I: Glossarium. (A Latin-to-English word list.)
  • Teach and Learn Latin Online: Lingua Latina: Chapter-by-Chapter Help Reading Familia Romana: worksheets and other study aids for the book Lingua Latina Per Se Illustrata.
  • Vocabulary for Lingua Latina Per Se Illustrata on Memrise.
  • Sylvānus Pūblius: Lingua Latina: YouTube playlist with over 130 videos where texts from Lingua Latina are read aloud.
  • Vitae et Scholae - De vita et methodo Iohannis H. Ørbergii (1910–2010): 30-minute YouTube video—in Latin—about the life and work of Hans H. Ørberg.

  • NativLang: What Latin Sounded Like - and How We Know (6 minutes, YouTube, 12.08.2016).
  • Anja Never Down: Vorbereitung Latinum mündlich: a YouTube playlist containing 30 videos from various channels. The Latinum is an official Latin proficiency test.
  • Schola Latina Catilina in Germany offers distance courses in Latin. The school also has a YouTube channel.

Other Latin Resources

  • Digital Latin Library: a project with the following mission:
    1. To publish and curate critical editions of Latin texts, of all types, from all eras, and to facilitate an ongoing scholarly conversation about these texts through open collaboration and annotation.
    2. To facilitate the finding and, where openly available and accessible online, the reading of all texts written in Latin.
    The project also has a Twitter account.
  • The Latin Quarter.
  • Melissa: a magazine entirely in Latin published by the Melissa Foundation, which also a Latin speaking group in Brussels, Belgium.
